Productivity Hacks for a 9–5 Professional

🌙
The Night-Before 10-Minute Plan

Every evening, write 3 specific tasks for tomorrow's session. Not "practice Python" — but "Complete Kaggle pandas lesson 4 and redo the groupby exercise." Decision fatigue is the silent killer of morning productivity.

⏰
Protect 6–7:30am Like a Boardroom Meeting

This is your most important learning window. No WhatsApp, no email, no news. Think of it as a standing appointment with the version of you who has the remote job — he's waiting.

🍱
Lunch Break = SQL Practice

30 minutes of StrataScratch or SQLZoo every lunch. Consistent 30-minute daily sessions over 6 weeks will outperform sporadic 3-hour weekend cramming every single time.

📤
The Friday Commit Rule

Every Friday evening, push something to GitHub — even a half-finished notebook. Public commit history shows recruiters a pattern of consistent, real work. One month of Fridays already beats most applicants.

📋
Batch Job Applications — Saturday Morning Only

Block 1.5 hours every Saturday morning exclusively for job search: research roles, tailor CVs, write cover letters, send applications. Batching stops the anxiety bleeding into study time.

📄
Your Preprint Is a Door Opener

When reaching out to hiring managers on LinkedIn, reference your Research Square preprint and your Streamlit app. Not everyone applying for healthcare analytics roles has published research in the field. You do.
